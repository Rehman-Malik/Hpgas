Do's & Don'ts

Essential LPG Safety Rules

Following these guidelines can prevent accidents and save lives

DO's

  • Always keep the gas cylinder in an upright position
  • Ensure proper ventilation in the kitchen at all times
  • Turn off the regulator knob when gas is not in use
  • Check hose pipe for cracks and replace every 2 years
  • Keep cylinders away from heat sources
  • Verify the seal is intact when taking delivery
  • Use only ISI marked regulators and hose pipes
  • Call 1906 in case of any gas emergency
  • Get your LPG equipment checked annually

DON'Ts

  • Never use a naked flame to detect gas leaks
  • Do not store the cylinder near heat sources or flames
  • Never use a damaged or leaking cylinder
  • Do not use a cylinder that is lying on its side
  • Never tamper with the safety valve
  • Do not use non-standard or locally made pipes
  • Never leave the gas on while sleeping
  • Do not use electrical switches if you smell gas
  • Never attempt to refill cylinders at home
Emergency Protocol

What To Do If You Smell Gas

1

Don't Panic

Stay calm and act methodically. Panicking can cause more harm.

2

Turn Off Regulator

Immediately turn off the regulator knob on the cylinder.

3

Open Windows

Open all doors and windows for maximum ventilation.

4

No Electrical Switches

Don't switch on/off any lights or electrical appliances.

5

Call 1906

Call the emergency helpline from outside the house.
